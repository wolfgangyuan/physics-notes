Explanation of The Classifications

The first classification concerns the status of completion, which has three cases: not added, incomplete, and completed. These are represented respectively by strikethrough text, gray links, and blue links.

The second classification is based on the breadth of content, as reflected in the three main headings on the homepage. Subject notes refer to notes on a subdiscipline. They are relatively self-contained and comprehensive. Topic notes focus on a specific theme, usually at the level of a course rather than frontier research. Their scope is narrower, but they are widely useful across many research areas. Research notes are dedicated to a particular research direction, with an even narrower scope but greater depth. Thus, from subject to topic to research notes, the breadth decreases while the depth increases.

The third classification concerns the mode of organization, also in three forms: record-style notes, logical notes, and historical notes. The first type records material while reading textbooks or papers or attending lectures, sometimes including detailed derivations. The second type is written after finishing a topic, arranged step by step in a logical order, often as a personal summary. The third type is structured along the historical development of an idea, usually motivated by an interest in its origins and exploration process. These three styles often appear successively as one's study progresses.
